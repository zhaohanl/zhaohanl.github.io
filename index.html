My research interests lie in behavioral economics, market dynamics, and the role of policy in economic development. I'm particularly intrigued by how psychological factors influence economic decision-making and the ways in which markets adjust to changes in policy and global economic trends.

Fiscal Stimulus and Economic Recovery: An Econometric Analysis of Post-Recession Periods

AP/ECON 2400, 2024 W

For the macroeconomics course project, I conducted an in-depth analysis of the effects of fiscal stimulus on economic recovery in post-recession periods. Using a combination of econometric models and case studies of recent economic downturns, I examined how different types of government spending and tax policies influenced GDP growth, employment rates, and consumer spending. This project allowed me to apply theoretical concepts to practical scenarios, enhancing my ability to interpret economic data and policy implications.
